Black Hole

A black hole is an extremely dense region of space whose gravitational pull is so intense that nothing—not even light—can escape. They form when massive stars collapse under gravity, leaving a singularity (a point of infinite density) surrounded by an event horizon, the "point of no return".

Key Properties

Event Horizon & Singularity: The event horizon marks where escape velocity equals the speed of light. Inside this boundary, all paths lead inward to the singularity, where physics breaks down

Types:
Stellar‑mass: A few to tens of solar masses, formed by collapsing stars.
Supermassive: Millions to billions of solar masses.
Intermediate: Hundreds to thousands of solar masses; their origins remain under study

Spaghettification: Extreme tidal forces stretch and compress objects entering a black hole—stretching them like spaghetti when approaching the event horizon

Hawking Radiation: A theoretical quantum effect where black holes gradually lose mass via radiation—observable only over timescales far exceeding the universe's age for large black holes

Recent Discoveries & Insights

Event Horizon Telescope (EHT):

2025 EHT Update: Astronomers identified why M87’s ring-shaped shadow is not a perfect circle—tracing distortions to plasma turbulence and the black hole’s spin orientation AI-Enhanced Imaging: Machine learning has enabled refined reconstructions of Sagittarius A*, revealing that it rotates near maximum spin and points toward Earth—challenging earlier moderately fast rotation estimates.

Tidal Disruption Events (TDEs): In early 2025, Hubble tracked a wandering black hole ripping apart a star outside its galaxy’s core, offering new glimpses into black hole feeding behavior.

Summary

Black holes represent both fascination and frontiers: invisible objects defined by their shadows, extreme gravitational physics, and quantum mysteries. With cutting-edge imaging, machine learning, and more telescopic data entering the picture, they're transforming our grasp of the universe—and challenging our theories to keep up.
